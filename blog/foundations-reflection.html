Foundations Reflection

Reflection of my learning throughout foundations core

Three new things I learnt about myself and my ego due to the core learning?

A few things I learnt about myself is that I am a good individual learner and have an open mindset. Throughout primary school and highschool I had learnt about having an open mindset and implementing it into my daily life. This has helped me with stress and anxiety, I am also able to see the positive on a situation if I am stuck on a problem or something personal has gone wrong.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

The role of values, empathy and self-awareness in terms of learning and programming is how you outlook programming. If you value your friends and family and want to see them make time for it and adjust your learning and programming around your values and morales. Being self-aware will make you understand that a particular problem may be to hard and you need to take back and re-think or ask for help. These all help you to stay true to yourself.

What has surprised me the most about the core learning?

Just learning about myself and how much being open and true to myself can improve my personal health. By being open and wanting to learn makes learning more enjoyable and applicable, where I am able to put my learning into practice quicker and at a faster pace.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Me wanting to get into tech and learn about code. I like doing the core work and I see the importance of it but I just want to get into coding and making programs that function on a screen. Just my personal preference.

Why do I think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because getting a job in the tech industry isn't about who can write the best code, although that is an added bonus, having good people skills is the most important. Being able to communicate with others and yourself is something employers look for when hiring.

Does the time I spend studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should I have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't feel like it is a waste of time. However, I am fresh out of highschool and have been learning about personal wellbeing and social wellbeing throughout the past 6 years so I am getting a little bit sick of it. As I said earlier I just want to get into the tech and code and see my learning into practice. But, if I was older and hadn't been taught about an open mindset, neuroplasticity and emotional-intelligence then I would certainly be interested in earning it.
