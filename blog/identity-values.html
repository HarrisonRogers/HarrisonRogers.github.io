Identity and Values

Learning my identity and values

A situation where I made an ethical decision.

When I was playing rugby I went on holiday overseas during the school holidays, the team had training through the holidays and I wasn’t able to attend any practices. I arrived back from holiday on a Friday and we had a game on the Saturday despite not being at training. I was still named to the starting team. I thought it was quite unfair since there was another strong player in my position who had attended all the training throughout the holidays so he deserved that spot more than I did. I told the coach that he should start since I won’t be as fit and it is unfair on him and to the team as well. In the end, another player started over me.

I believe this came from the values I have of fairness and caring. It was only fair that he started over me since he had been doing the mahi I hadn’t been, and it was my job to get that position back. I positively reflect on the outcome of that decision because not only was I living up to my values but I was also able to give myself competition and a drive to succeed to secure my spot back in the starting team.

Describe how my culture and the people around me have influenced my values and identity.

The people who influence me are the people I am closest to, like my family. Throughout my parents and brothers' life, it has been full of success. Success in the sporting and business world. I have grown up with a lot of people around me working hard to achieve their goals and mostly succeeding them through the hard work that they put in. I have learnt many things from the people around me which has certainly shaped my values and identity for the better by making me a harder worker, having a desire to win, having fairness and being disciplined.

My strengths and how they will support me during my learning journey.

After taking the VIA character quiz it defined my top 5 strengths as…

  1. Self Regulation - Regulating what one feels and does; being disciplined; controlling one's appetites and emotions.
  2. Hope - Expecting the best in the future and working to achieve it; believing that a good future is something that can be brought about.
  3. Humility - Letting one's accomplishments speak for themselves; not regarding oneself as more special than one is.
  4. Honesty - Speaking the truth but more broadly genuinely presenting oneself and acting sincerely; being without pretense; taking responsibility for one's feelings and actions.
  5. Humour - Liking to laugh and tease; bringing smiles to other people; seeing the light side; making (not necessarily telling) jokes.

My top strength of self-regulation I believe will help me a lot since I do feel like I am a very disciplined person and am quite good at controlling my emotions especially if I am in a stressful situation. During Highschool I was never stressed about any school work and the deadlines ahead which made me calm and able to have quite a calm ride through school. I also feel like the hope strength will benefit me greatly because I am excited for my future and to be able to achieve what I want in my future I must work for it which gives me more motivation and drive with Dev Academy to help me achieve my goals.

My limitations might be my inexperience in a working environment. I have only just graduated high school and I’m pretty sure I’m the youngest in the course so the long hours of working and having to stay motivated will be a new experience for me because I’m only used to the 6-hour school timeline. But I’m hoping my youthfulness can give me the energy and motivation to adapt to the new normal.

An example of when I was trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

Playing rugby there is a lot of adrenaline which for some people can make their temper have a short fuse even for their teammates. When playing a game against a high-level team it was difficult enough as it was since we were getting beaten by a big private school when two of our teammates had a miscommunication and started blaming each other. It got very heated and a conflict was caused that needed to be contained. My teammates and I took one of the players each and tried to calm them down and explain that there are bigger and worse things that he should be worrying about than a silly miscommunication. It calmed him down and they hugged it out and continued.

The strategy of calming down and explaining the problem helped them evaluate the situation and come to the realisation that the conflict was pretty pointless and to move on and continue. I wouldn’t try another strategy in that situation because it worked and it made a positive outcome.
